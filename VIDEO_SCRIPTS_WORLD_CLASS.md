## **PRODUCTION NOTES**

### **Screen Flow (Recommended Order)**

For any script, show screens in this flow:

1. **Opening problem** (patient waiting, clinician overwhelmed)
2. **Patient experience** (chat interface, mood tracking)
3. **AI response** (show actual AI conversation)
4. **Clinician view** (dashboard, alerts, summary)
5. **Safety/risk detection** (C-SSRS flag, alert system)
6. **Outcomes** (metrics, testimonial, improvement)
7. **Call-to-action** (contact info, calendar link)

### **Tone & Delivery Tips**

**DO:**
- Speak like you believe this (because you do)
- Pause after big statements (let them sink in)
- Use patient names if possible ("Sarah got help at 11 PM...")
- Show genuine emotion (you lost someone to suicide—that's real)
- Vary pacing (slow for emotional beats, faster for momentum)

**DON'T:**
- Rush through numbers (let stats breathe)
- Sound robotic or pitch-like (conversational, peer-to-peer)
- Oversell features (focus on outcomes, not features)
- Be too clinical (mix technical + emotional)
- Fake emotion (authenticity > polish)

### **Camera/Audio Setup**

If recording yourself (D-ID or on webcam):
- Good lighting (window light is fine, no harsh shadows)
- Quiet room (close doors, use headphones if nearby noise)
- Speak to the camera like you're talking to one person (David, Claire, a patient)
- One take is fine—imperfection = authenticity

If doing voiceover (Loom screen recording):
- Use a quiet space
- Speak to the screen like you're walking someone through
- Click/highlight as you speak
- One clean take is better than jumbled perfection
